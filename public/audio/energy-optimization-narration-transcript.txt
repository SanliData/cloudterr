Data center energy optimization relies on two innovative processes: natural gas combined cycle absorption and subterranean heat exchange. The combined cycle absorption technology captures and converts high-grade waste heat into mechanical or cooling energy, eliminating the need for fossil fuels and increasing energy efficiency. For hyperscale data centers, a subterranean system utilizes the earth's natural thermal properties to store and retrieve thermal energy at varying temperatures for cooling and heating purposes. With subterranean heat exchange, ground-source heat pumps facilitate heat transfer between the data center and the stable-temperature ground, which is much more efficient than using ambient air. Together, combined cycle absorption and subterranean heat exchange technologies pave the way for sustainable and responsible data center operations.
